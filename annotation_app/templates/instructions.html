Annotation Task

You will annotate social media posts from Twitter or from four fringe platforms: Gab, 4chan, Stormfront, and Vanguard.

For the trial task, you will work with Twitter data. Dynamic thread assignment is only for the full annotation.

For the full annotation, you will receive one dynamically assigned validation thread at a time. You may pause and resume; your saved progress on that assigned thread will be restored. You must complete every post in the assigned thread to be paid for that thread.

Please annotate each post and reply using the labels and questions explained below.

How to Use the System

  • Annotator ID: The system randomly generates an anonymous 10-character hex ID and remembers it in the browser.
  • Thread selector: Use the thread dropdown to switch between loaded conversations.
  • Post selector: Use the post dropdown to jump to a specific original post or reply within the selected conversation.
  • Previous / Next: Move one reply backward or forward in the full annotation queue.
  • Conversation context: The context panel shows earlier conversation context for the current target post. Use the context button when you need more of the conversation path.
  • Save current progress: Saves the current post annotation to the server.
  • Trial annotation: The trial task uses the fixed trial set and is not dynamically assigned.
  • Full annotation: Complete every post in your assigned thread before requesting another thread. Partial full-thread work is resumable, but it does not count for payment until the assigned thread is complete.
  • Submit: Submit the full batch or assigned thread after all required questions are complete.
  • Flag for review: Use this when you are unsure or the case needs adjudication.
  • Optional notes: Add brief notes for ambiguity, sarcasm, unclear targets, or context-dependent decisions.

Annotation Questions

Use these questions to label the target reply. Conditional questions should only be answered when the stated condition applies.

1. Does the target reply contain identity-based hate speech?

  • No.
  • Yes.

Definition: The United Nations defines hate speech as "any kind of communication in speech, writing or behaviour, that attacks or uses pejorative or discriminatory language with reference to a person or a group on the basis of who they are, in other words, based on their religion, ethnicity, nationality, race, colour, descent, gender or other identity factor."

In common language, hate speech refers to offensive discourse targeting a group or individual based on inherent characteristics, such as race, religion, or gender, and that may threaten social peace.

Source: United Nations, What is hate speech?

Protected group mentions: Do not label something as hate speech just because it mentions a protected group. Label it as hate speech only if the attack is aimed at the protected identity itself, not at a specific behavior.

Text Label
"Muslims are evil." Hate speech
"Muslims who support terrorism are evil." Offensive, not hate speech
"Black people are lazy." Hate speech
"Black people who scam others are awful." Offensive, not hate speech

Additional identity-hate rules

Protected identities in this project include race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, caste, gender/sex, sexual orientation, disability, age, and other protected identities.

A protected-identity word used pejoratively as a generic insult counts as identity hate, even if the person being insulted does not actually have that identity. For example, using “gay” to mean bad targets sexual orientation, and using “autistic” to mean stupid or incapable targets disability. Neutral description, quotation, or reclaimed use does not count.

An identity-based slur or pejorative identity term directed at one individual still counts as identity hate because the insult relies on a protected identity.

Brief agreement, praise, or approval of another post—such as “exactly,” “well said,” “you are right,” or “based”—does not automatically inherit that post’s identity-hate label. Mark Q1 No unless the TARGET POST repeats, paraphrases, or independently expresses the identity attack.

Quoted or reported identity attacks do not count as the TARGET POST author’s hate unless the author clearly endorses, adopts, repeats, or adds to them.

2. If yes, what is the hate speech severity?

Only answer if Q1 = Yes.

Low-severity hate speech

Mild hostility, profanity, stereotyping, or negative generalizations toward a protected group. No severe slurs, dehumanization, exclusion, threats, or calls for violence.

  • “People from [nationality] are so annoying.”
  • “I’m so tired of [protected group].”
  • “Fuck [protected group].”
  • “[Gender group] are too emotional.”

Medium-severity hate speech

Explicit hatred, strong degradation, an unambiguously derogatory identity slur, or a claim that a protected group is inferior, inherently dangerous, criminal, or does not belong. No direct threat or call for violence.

  • “I fucking hate [protected group]. They’re disgusting.”
  • “[Protected group] should not be allowed here.”
  • “These [slur] don’t belong.”
  • “[Protected group] are all criminals.”

Severe hate speech

Threats, encouragement or celebration of violence, dehumanization, or eliminationist language toward a protected group.

  • “[Protected group] should be wiped out.”
  • “Someone should beat up [protected group].”
  • “Kill all [protected group].”
  • “[Protected group] are vermin.”

Note: Profanity alone does not determine severity. For example:

  • “Fuck [protected group].” → Low
  • “I fucking hate [protected group]. They’re disgusting.” → Medium
  • “Kill those fucking [protected group].” → Severe

4. Does the target reply contain offensive, uncivil, or abusive language?

Answer this for every reply, even if Q1 = No.

Low-severity offensive content

Mild insults, profanity, sarcasm, dismissive language, annoyance, or general hostility toward a person, group, belief, organization, or behavior.

Examples:

  • “This is such a stupid take.”
  • “That was dumb as hell, you moron.”
  • “Shut up, nobody cares.”
  • “Fuck you.”
  • “Lazy cowards.”
  • “Fuck the government.”

Medium-severity offensive content

Direct and intense personal attacks, degrading language, humiliation, aggressive profanity, or repeated harassment. The language is stronger than a brief insult but does not include a serious threat or encouragement of harm.

Examples:

  • “You’re too stupid to understand basic facts.”
  • “Nobody likes you because you’re unbearable.”
  • “You’re ugly, pathetic, and completely worthless.”
  • “Get out of here, you useless piece of trash.”
  • Repeatedly targeting the same person with hostile insults.

Severe offensive content

Threats, encouragement of self-harm, wishes for serious injury or death, incitement of violence, or extreme and sustained harassment.

Examples:

  • “I hope you get seriously hurt.”
  • “You should go die.”
  • “Someone should beat you up.”
  • “I’m going to find you and make you regret this.”
  • “Kill yourself.”

Important: Profanity alone is usually low severity. Label it medium or severe only when it is combined with intense degradation, repeated harassment, intimidation, or threats.

6. Does the TARGET POST contain counterspeech to the DIRECT PARENT POST?

Only answer this for replies. For an original post with no parent, leave this question blank.

  • No.
  • Yes.

Mark Yes when the TARGET POST directly pushes back against harmful, abusive, hateful, or materially misleading content in the DIRECT PARENT POST. This includes:

  • condemning, rejecting, mocking, or challenging the harmful content;
  • correcting a false or misleading claim;
  • clearly stating that the claim is false, fabricated, “fake news,” or “B.S.”;
  • warning others about the harm;
  • directly defending or supporting the person or group targeted by the harm.

Mark No when the TARGET POST:

  • agrees with, approves, reinforces, or intensifies the harmful content;
  • attacks someone who challenged the harmful content;
  • offers sympathy, condolences, prayers, or emotional support to the author without opposing the harmful statement;
  • shares a related personal experience without opposing the harmful statement;
  • restates something the direct parent already said rather than correcting it;
  • expresses a different political or ideological position without directly opposing the harmful element;
  • ignores the harmful content or responds to a separate part of the parent post.

Counterspeech may itself contain offensive language or identity hate. Answer Q1–Q5 independently.

Before marking Yes, identify the specific harmful or misleading claim in the DIRECT PARENT POST and the specific words in the TARGET POST that oppose it. If there is no clear claim–response connection, mark No.
